## Economic Model

### Loyalty Liability (IFRS 15 / ASC 606)
- "Material right" = distinct performance obligation
- Allocate transaction price to points based on standalone selling price
- Deferred revenue until redemption or expiry
- Disclosure: SSP judgment, redemption rate, contract liability balance + roll-forward, breakage method
- [PwC ASC 606/IFRS 15 loyalty guide](https://www.pwc.com/us/en/industries/financial-services/library/loyalty-program-managers-asc606-ifrs15.html)

### Breakage Rate Benchmarks
| Sector | Typical breakage |
|---|---|
| Cashback | ~5% |
| CPG / general retail | 20-30% |
| Travel / airline FFP | >40% (some 70-85%) |
| B2B incentive | 70-85% |

Healthy programs target 10-20%. 1% miss on $1B liability = $10M P&L hit.

### Float Economics (Banking-Like Profit Pool)
- Starbucks 2021: ~$155M non-taxable profit from unused balances ("a banking business with coffee on the side")
- Q2 2024: $1.872B in stored-value liabilities
- US gift-card market 2025: $447B revenue; $23B unused (43% of US adults)

### Plusgrade Monetization
- Points buy/top-up + exchange/transfer + SaaS fees
- Currency Alliance benchmark: ~2% of points value exchanged

### Why Issue Points: CAC vs LTV
- HBS: +5% retention = +25-95% profits
- Wharton: referred customers +25% margin
- LTV:CAC ≥ 3:1
- Bond Loyalty Report: avg consumer in 19 programs
- 80% of consumers buy more from brands with programs
- McKinsey: top programs lift redeemer revenue 15-25% annually
- BCG: highly personalized programs lift purchases +110%, spending +40%

### Airlines = "Credit Cards With Wings"
- AAdvantage ~$30B vs AAL market cap <$15B at points
- Delta: 80%+ of revenue tied to loyalty + premium 2024
- United: $22B at 12× EBITDA
- Banks prepurchase miles ~2¢ each; airlines redeem at <1¢ — spread × scale

## Pain Points

### User-Side
- **Devaluation** (CFPB May 2024 + Circular 2024-07): unilateral revaluation, hidden conditions, revocation, redemption obstacles
- **Cross-program friction**: only Avios sub-network + a few bank-to-airline transfers
- **Expiration anxiety**: Bond — biggest complaint is "too long to earn reward"
- **Fraud**: ~$3.1B/yr stolen value; 72% of programs hit; 89% YoY in 2024

### Merchant-Side
- **Liability inflation when redemption rises**: PC Optimum $129M Q4 2024 charge
- **Coalition coordination failure**: Plenti 2018
- **Co-brand bank profitability stress**: Wells Fargo-Bilt $10M/month
- **Data silos**
- **Regulatory tightening**: CFPB Circular 2024-07
- **Escheatment**

### Industry-Wide
- Average consumer in 19 programs (over-saturation)
- 71% expect personalization, only 22% deliver (McKinsey)
- ~$360B unredeemed (defensible figure; "$48T total notional" widely circulated but methodology unclear)
- Trust deficit reinforced by Marriott 2024-25, Delta 2023, Hyatt periodic devaluations

## Strategic Implications

1. Loyalty = financial-services business inside a brand wrapper
2. Third-party currency consolidation: Plusgrade incumbent
3. Coalition models work in Europe/Japan/Korea/Canada/Australia, fail in US
4. China's structure: state/super-app dominance; less SEC-style disclosure; walled gardens
5. Regulatory direction: CFPB + ACCC tightening on devaluation
6. AI/personalization is next investment wave (32% prioritized 2024)
